POETRY

she has been cast as the Inevitable Blooming Onion.
she makes her entrance.
lights up, center stage right.
a single spotlight
‘here i am!’
blooming onion: inevitable unravel, she precedes with an audience
having no eyes
they are left only to open their mouths
i sit on my hands, mouth open watching her
i am now clogging my mouth with paper, the same paper they handed everyone else
there is more than one drop of accompanied blood
this little girl inside me : thats where she lives, wonders why, in constant
now the shows over
she takes my hands out of my mouth, telling me how beautiful i am.
“i feel beautiful” i tell her
but then she asks why?
does she even need to know why?
taking my bow at the smallest of applause
i am met with the same little girl, only she has a razor blade in her hand
this time she has stepped outside of me
this girl has given me one motion,
this little girl still stabbing me with metal corners
my scales are broken exposing my fleshy insides,
i am in the constant ask of why.

if she was to start again,
as she’s doing now,
her ankles would fall through the
ground,
planted she is no longer expelling upwards,
towards Bethlehem,
fresh
the pair of lungs digest the old burning
she breathes again
giving birth
to the nameless
